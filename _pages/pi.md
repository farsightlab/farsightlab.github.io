## The lab

The **FarSight Lab**, led by Professor [Lorenzo Torresani](#pi), develops AI systems for perception, anticipation, and planning in dynamic worlds. Our research spans egocentric video understanding for wearable assistants, large-scale learning from noisy video corpora, and strategic reasoning over multi-agent scenes such as team sports.

A common thread across our work is the move *beyond perception toward strategic understanding*: building models that not only observe dynamic scenes but reason about cause and consequence, simulate alternative futures, and plan over long horizons. We design models that learn directly from rich signals present in raw video, and benchmarks that test whether models truly understand what they see.

His contributions include pioneering methods for 3D reconstruction of non-rigid objects and influential architectures for image and video analysis (Classemes, C3D, TimeSformer). At Meta, he co-led the **Ego-Exo4D** project, a two-year effort coordinating over 100 researchers across 15 university partners worldwide that produced the leading benchmark for AI understanding of skilled human activity. He has published over 90 peer-reviewed conference papers, with an h-index of 69, and his most-referenced paper (**C3D**) has been cited more than 12,000 times.

Among his recognitions are a **CVPR best student paper prize**, an **NSF CAREER Award**, a **Google Faculty Research Award**, three **Facebook Faculty Awards**, and a **Fulbright U.S. Scholar Award**.

In his free time, Lorenzo is an avid off-pavement cyclist and enjoys self-supported multi-day bikepacking in the wilderness.
